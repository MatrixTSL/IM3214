1. Introduction

In fault critical systems, such as industrial processes where a fault could have catastrophic consequences, it is important to track all problems as they develop. When using analogue sensors, it can be difficult to detect a faulty reading. If a sensor is short-circuited to one of the power supply rails, the input signal would still resemble a valid signal.

3. So What?

Intelligent Fault Detection

Well-designed circuits and control logic play a key role in ensuring equipment operates safely and reliably. Intelligent use of inputs and monitoring can help detect faults early, confirm devices are responding correctly, and reduce unplanned downtime.

Range-Based Detection

By defining valid operating ranges for analogue signals, systems can automatically detect when sensors are short-circuited to power rails or experiencing other failures that would otherwise appear as valid readings.

Safety Standards

This approach is especially important when maintaining systems that must meet strict safety or operational standards. Early fault detection prevents cascading failures and maintains system integrity.

Key Takeaways

  • Range-based fault detection identifies sensor failures automatically.
  • Short circuits to power rails can appear as valid signals without range checking.
  • Valid operating ranges prevent false readings from being accepted.
  • Early fault detection reduces unplanned downtime and improves safety.
  • Intelligent monitoring is essential for safety-critical systems.

Questions & Answers

Question 1: Why is range-based fault detection important for analogue sensors?

Correct Answer: C) To detect sensor failures that would otherwise appear as valid signals.

Question 2: What happens when a sensor signal is outside the valid range of 50-200?

Correct Answer: A) The red fault LED turns on.

Question 3: Why is a signal converter needed with an RTD sensor?

Correct Answer: B) Because the PLC can't read resistance directly—it needs a 0–10 V voltage signal.

Question 4: What does the PLC do with the analogue voltage from the sensor?

Correct Answer: D) It converts the voltage into a raw value, then multiplies it by a scalar to get temperature in °C.

Question 5: What might a raw value near 32,000 mean?

Correct Answer: C) It usually means a fault like a disconnected sensor, wrong input type, or wiring issue.

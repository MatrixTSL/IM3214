Worksheet 7 - Using Sensors

1. Introduction

Some sensors are digital, such as reed relays, Hall effect sensors, and simple level sensors. However, most are analogue, their output signal varies between 0 and 10V usually, representing a varying quantity like light level, temperature, or pressure. We can use signals from these to measure quantities and make control decisions.

3. So What?

Analogue Signal Importance

Being able to read and interpret analogue signals is essential in industrial maintenance. Most real-world values like temperature, pressure, and flow vary continuously.

Greater Accuracy

Analogue sensors provide continuous information, allowing systems to respond with greater accuracy. Understanding how these signals work helps maintenance teams troubleshoot faults and calibrate equipment.

Safe Operation

Monitoring analogue sensors ensures systems operate within safe and efficient limits. Temperature monitoring prevents overheating, pressure sensors prevent over-pressurization, and flow sensors detect blockages.

Key Takeaways

  • Analogue sensors provide continuous variable signals (0-10V) unlike digital sensors.
  • Temperature, pressure, and flow are typically measured with analogue sensors.
  • Monitoring analogue values enables predictive maintenance and early fault detection.
  • Understanding sensor signals helps troubleshoot equipment and prevent failures.
  • Calibration and regular checks ensure accurate sensor readings.

Questions & Answers

Question 1: What is the main difference between digital and analogue sensors?

Correct Answer: B) Analogue sensors provide variable voltage signals representing continuous values, while digital sensors provide on/off signals.

Question 2: What typically happens when a thermistor detects a 1% temperature increase?

Correct Answer: A) The green status LED turns off and the red error LED turns on.

Question 3: What type of object does an inductive sensor detect?

Correct Answer: B) Metal objects only.

Question 4: What caused the fault in the locking sequence during the exercise?

Correct Answer: D) The system couldn’t detect the locking pins because the sensor was not triggered.

Question 5: How can engineers test a proximity sensor during troubleshooting?

Correct Answer: B) By placing a metal object in front of the sensor to simulate detection.

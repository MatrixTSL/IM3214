1. Introduction

A switch typically sends either a 24V or 0V signal to a controller input. A potentiometer, on the other hand, provides a variable voltage between 0V and 24V. Potentiometers are useful for setting values like light level, conveyor speed, or room temperature.

3. So What?

Why this matters

Understanding the difference between NO and NC is critical in maintenance. On I/O or mimic screens, you need to know whether an input should be on or off when the button is not pressed. For NO contacts, the input shows off until pressed; for NC, the input is on until pressed. If you don’t know this, you might think there’s a wiring fault when there isn’t.

Direct Panel Adjustment

A common and simple method is to use a dial, typically a potentiometer, which allows maintenance teams to adjust settings directly on the panel without needing to access the software.

How to verify quickly

Use the HMI IO page and press the device: NO should change from off→on, NC from on→off. If it doesn’t, check wiring, device, or PLC input channel.

Key Takeaways

  • Potentiometers provide variable voltage between 0V and 24V.
  • Variable inputs are essential for setting operational parameters.
  • Direct panel adjustment improves maintenance efficiency.
  • Voltage thresholds can trigger different system responses.
  • Analogue inputs enable precise control in industrial systems.

Questions & Answers

Question 1: What type of signal does a potentiometer provide compared to a switch?

Correct Answer: B) Variable voltage between 0V and 24V, unlike a switch which provides 24V or 0V.

Question 2: What are potentiometers commonly used for in industrial systems?

Correct Answer: C) Setting values like light level, conveyor speed, or room temperature.

Question 3: What advantage do potentiometers provide to maintenance teams?

Correct Answer: B) They allow direct panel adjustment without needing to access software.

Question 4: In the heater control example, what happens when the voltage exceeds 5V?

Correct Answer: C) The green LED turns off and the red LED turns on.

Question 5: Why is it important to understand NO and NC input states in maintenance?

Correct Answer: A) So you can correctly read input states and avoid misdiagnosing faults.
